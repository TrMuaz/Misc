Human Perceptions & Emotions

Islamic Educational Psychology · Lesson Note
Qur'an Hadith Classical Scholars Islamic Psychology

This lesson note explores six core human emotions through the framework of Islamic educational psychology. Every discussion draws on the Qur'ān, prophetic Hadith, and insights from classical Muslim scholars, then connects to practical implications for the classroom. Select an emotion below to begin.

😨
الخَوْف — Khawf

Fear

Fear protects humans from various problems and threats. In Islam, fear is not always interpreted as feeling threatened or anxious — it also includes a sense of reverence and awe for the power of Allah.

Overview

Islam emphasises fear of Allah as a form of obedience and submission, encouraging individuals to surrender themselves and not fear anything other than Him.

Among all these, fear of Allah is the most important because it brings good outcomes and leads to safety in this life and the Hereafter.

Qur'anAl-Anfal: 2
"Indeed, the believers are those whose hearts tremble when Allah is mentioned. Moreover, when His verses are recited to them, their faith increases, and upon their Lord they rely."
Al-Anfal: 2
Qur'anQuraish: 4
"Who has given them food to relieve their hunger and secure them from fear."
Quraish: 4
HadithHR. Tirmidhi
"The heat of Hell will not touch anyone who cries out of fear of Allah until the milk returns to its udder."
Narrated from Abu Hurairah · HR. Tirmidhi

💭 Reflection

Islam emphasises fear of Allah as a form of obedience and submission. Fear of Allah brings good outcomes and leads to safety in this life and the Hereafter. Ask students: write a personal du'ā transforming worldly fear into reverential khawf towards Allah.

😤
الغَضَب — Ghadab

Anger

Anger is a natural human emotion, but dangerous when left uncontrolled. It is closely linked to conflict, retaliation, and emotional instability.

ScholarImam an-Nawawi
"Excessive anger leads to bigger problems."
Imam an-Nawawi
ScholarIbn Qayyim al-Jauziyah

Anger is a strong feeling that pushes a person to follow their desires. When someone gets angry, it affects their heart and can be seen on their face. This anger can also lead to feelings like hatred or envy towards others.

Ibn Qayyim al-Jauziyah
Psychological AccountImam Al-Ghazali
"The ghadab is like a fierce animal. If you let him, he will eat you."
Imam Al-Ghazali

When a person's anger flares up, blood flow in the heart rushes to the brain, controlling all thoughts and even the source of goodness. The eyes darken so that the person cannot see clearly. At that moment, the brain is like a cave spreading fire, blackening the atmosphere and enveloping all sides with smoke. The fire of anger can destroy the heart and kill the individual's existence without them realising it.

Every time anger arises, Satan begins to act to ignite emotions to their peak. Therefore, it is appropriate for every Muslim who possesses reason to defeat Satan, restrain their anger, and understand the real situation.

Qur'anStory of Prophet Musa · Al-A'raf: 150
"When Moses returned to his people in anger and sadness, he said, 'How terrible is what you have done in my absence! Do you intend to preempt the promise of your Lord?' Moses threw down the tablets (the Torah) and grabbed his brother (Aaron) by the head, pulling him toward him. Aaron said, 'O son of my mother, these people have considered me weak, and they almost killed me. Therefore, do not let the enemies cheer me on. Do not make me (in your view) one of the wrongdoers.'"
Al-A'raf: 150
HadithMuttafaqun 'Alaih
"Indeed, strength is not shown by harshness, but strength is possessed by a person who can restrain himself when he is angry."
Muttafaqun 'Alaih

✏️ Class Activity

Practise the prophetic four-step method as a class routine when conflicts arise:

  • Stay silent
  • Sit down
  • Perform wudu
  • Seek refuge — recite isti'āzah (أَعُوذُ بِاللهِ مِنَ الشَّيْطَانِ الرَّجِيم)

الحُزْن — Huzn

Sadness

Sadness is an unhappy emotional state with intensity ranging from mild to extreme. It is associated with despair, grief, misery, feeling wronged, loss, helplessness, and disappointment. Islam acknowledges sadness with compassion and wisdom — even the greatest prophets experienced deep grief.

Qur'anProphet Ya'qub (Jacob) · Yusuf: 84
"He (Jacob) turned away from them (his children) and said, 'How pitiful is Joseph,' and his eyes turned white with grief. He was a man who truly restrained (his anger and pain)."
Yusuf: 84
Qur'anMother of Prophet Musa · Al-Qashash: 13
"Then We returned him (Musa) to his mother so that her heart would be glad and she would not grieve, and so that she would know that Allah's promise is true, but most of them do not know."
Al-Qashash: 13
Qur'anAbu Bakr ash-Shiddiq · At-Taubah: 40
"...when he said to his companion, 'Do not be sad, surely Allah is with us...'"
At-Taubah: 40
HadithHR. Bukhari & Muslim
"Have you not heard that Allah will not punish a person for tears or sadness in the heart, but He will punish a person for this (pointing to his tongue)?"
The Messenger of Allah ﷺ, upon visiting Sa'ad bin 'Ubadah · HR. Bukhari & Muslim

💭 Reflection

Crying and feeling sad are natural and permissible. Controlling sadness does not mean one cannot cry — it is permissible as long as it is not excessive and harmful, especially in relation to one's words and actions.

😔
الشُّعُور بِالنَّقص

Inferiority

Inferiority is when a person feels they have no value or purpose and believes they are not capable or useful. Even if others see them differently, they still feel unimportant inside.

Overview

Those who are unable to cope with feelings of inadequacy will experience a high sense of inferiority. In Islam, this condition is studied from a spiritual perspective — how faith and confidence in Allah can overcome these weaknesses. Prevention begins with strengthening faith, recognising the virtues given by Allah, and avoiding unhealthy comparisons with others.

Qur'anHuman Value & Dignity · At-Tin: 4
"Indeed, We have created man in the best form."
At-Tin: 4 — Humans are valuable and created perfectly by Allah, so they should not feel inferior.
Qur'anProhibition of Feeling Weak · Ali-'Imran: 139
"Do not feel weak and do not be sad, you will be superior if you are believers."
Ali-'Imran: 139 — Faith gives strength and confidence.
HadithProphet ﷺ
"We used to be the most humiliated people, then Allah honoured us with Islam."
Prophet ﷺ — True dignity comes from Islam and faith, not worldly status.
PrincipleIslamic Balance

Islam teaches balance in how we see ourselves. A Muslim should not feel inferior or weak, but at the same time should not become arrogant or think they are better than others. Humans are created by Allah in the best form with intelligence, physical ability, and spiritual potential.

Confidence should be based on faith (īmān), not on showing superiority over others.

💭 Reflection

Use At-Tin: 4 and Ali-'Imran: 139 in activities affirming each student's strengths. Avoid classroom comparisons that shame weaker students. Prevention begins with strengthening faith and recognising the virtues given by Allah.

😊
الحَيَاء — Hayā'

Shyness

Ḥayā' is an emotion that leads a person to maintain their honour and the honour of others. In Islam, shyness and shame are part of faith. Those who are resilient in difficult lives will be ashamed of their ability to commit evil.

Context

The sense of shame is slowly disappearing from society. Things that were once considered wrong and shameful are now seen as normal — for example, adultery becoming more common and even accepted as a lifestyle. There are also increasing cases of parents harming their own children and partners being unfaithful. Behaviours once strongly rejected are becoming more accepted in everyday life.

Qur'anAl-Ahzab: 53
"And when you ask them (the Prophet's wives) for something, ask them from behind a curtain."
Al-Ahzab: 53 — Al-Qurtubi: This verse teaches the importance of modesty in behaviour. Muslims should protect their honour, keep their hearts pure, and speak with good manners.
HadithHR. Bukhari
"Shyness is part of faith."
Prophet Muhammad ﷺ · HR. Bukhari
ScholarImam Al-Qurtubi
Modesty is an important part of faith because it helps protect a person's morals and keeps them away from doing wrong. It guides a person to stay pure and maintain their dignity. When a Muslim has a strong sense of modesty, they will feel hesitant to do anything that goes against the teachings of Allah and the Prophet, whether big or small.
Imam Al-Qurtubi
ScholarImam Ibn Hajar al-Asqalani (Fath al-Bari)
Shame or shyness is part of faith that guides a person to obey Allah always. It can trigger a person to maintain good behaviour and avoid evil deeds, ensuring that one's behaviour follows Islamic values.
Imam Ibn Hajar al-Asqalani
ScholarImam Al-Ghazali
Shame or shyness is a feeling that stops a person from doing bad things and encourages good behaviour. It is an important part of good morals (akhlāq). Muslims should feel ashamed to do wrong actions like adultery, gossiping, mocking others, or saying things that are not true. At the same time, they should not be shy to stand against wrongdoing or correct bad behaviour.
Imam Al-Ghazali

✏️ Class Activity

Clarify the distinction between "healthy shyness" (hayā' that prevents sin and protects honour) vs. "harmful shyness" (timidity that prevents one from speaking truth or correcting wrongdoing). Encourage students to be shy to sin but brave in upholding truth and justice.

😒
الحَسَد — Hasad

Envy

Envy characterises human life as psychologically disordered and always tends to do wrong to others. It emerges among people who do not adopt the lifestyle of friendship, respect for others, loving nature, happiness, and the philosophy of "live and let others live."

Overview

Envious people develop negativism, hatred, and prejudice without cause. Such people will never make themselves happy because they shape their psyche in a way that forces them to get irritated with others' progress and happiness.

Envy is harmful and can lead people to act dishonestly. It is one of the primary causes of problems in families, society, and even between countries. It weakens positive thinking and can lead to domestic violence, family abuse, and conflict in relationships.

Envy does not have clear external signs or symptoms. It comes from deep inside a person and manifests as dislike or rejection of others' good qualities and success. It is a dangerous condition of the heart and mind because it can lead to hypocrisy, backbiting, abuse, cursing, slander, mocking, and even hurting others.

Qur'anBrothers of Yusuf (AS) · Al-Qur'an 12:8–10
"The brothers said: 'Truly, Yusuf and his brother are loved more by our father than we, while we are a strong group. Our father is in a plain error. Kill Yusuf or cast him out to some other land, so that the favour of your father may be given to you alone, and after that, you will be righteous folk.' One among them said: 'Kill not Yusuf, but if you must do something, throw him down to the bottom of a well, he will be picked up by some caravan of travellers.'"
Al-Qur'an 12:8–10
HadithProphet's Warning Against Envy
"Do not be envious of one another; do not hate one another; do not artificially raise prices against one another; do not turn one's back on each other, and do not undercut one another in business transactions. And be, O servants of Allah, brethren. A Muslim is the brother of a Muslim. He does not belittle him. He does not forsake him. He does not betray him. And he does not show contempt for him. It is sufficient enough of evil for a person to despise his Muslim brother. Every Muslim's blood, wealth and honour is sacred."
Prophet Muhammad ﷺ
ScholarMaududi
Hasad means feeling unhappy when someone else has something good — like success or special qualities given by Allah — and wishing that those blessings are taken away from that person, or at least that they lose them.
Maududi
ScholarImam Al-Ghazali
Human beings have weaknesses that make them easily influenced by the devil, who can fill their hearts and minds with negative thoughts, leading to jealousy and prejudice. Islam strongly teaches Muslims to stay away from envy. Allah accepts the prayers of those who have pure hearts, free from jealousy and hatred.
Imam Al-Ghazali

💭 Reflection

Practise gratitude journaling and du'ā for others' success. Teach students to say "Mā shā' Allāh, tabārak Allāh" (مَا شَاءَ اللهُ تَبَارَكَ اللهُ) when seeing others' blessings — as protection against the evil eye and as an antidote to envy in the heart.

الخُلَاصَة — Implications

Pedagogical Implications

Emotions are not only individual experiences — they are educational opportunities. Islamic education can intentionally design teaching, environment, and assessment to form emotionally balanced and spiritually strong learners.

Principles for Educators
  • Use Qur'an, Hadith, and classical scholars alongside emotional psychology so students see emotions as both natural and guided by revelation.
  • Balance encouragement (hope, mercy, reward) with warning (consequences of uncontrolled anger, envy, and arrogance).
  • Teachers demonstrate calmness when provoked, humility instead of arrogance, and principled shyness — not weakness.
  • Invite students to share their feelings and connect them to Qur'anic stories — the sadness of Ya'qub, the fear of Musa's mother, the envy of Yusuf's brothers.
Classroom Strategies

✏️ Planning Task for Educators

Design a lesson or unit that integrates at least two of the six emotions discussed. Identify which Qur'anic verses, Hadith, or scholar insights you will use, and describe the classroom activity or assessment you will implement to develop students' emotional and spiritual literacy.

Summary

Human emotions such as fear, anger, shyness, inferiority, and envy are natural but must be guided by Islamic teachings. Fear of Allah builds obedience, while anger should be controlled to avoid harm.

Shyness is part of faith that protects dignity and prevents wrongdoing. Islam discourages inferiority, as humans are created in the best form and should have confidence through faith.

Envy is harmful and leads to negative behaviour, so Muslims must purify their hearts from it. In Islamic education, these teachings help develop good character, emotional control, and strong faith — shaping individuals to become better Muslims in this life and the Hereafter.
