What is Psychology?

Definitions, scope, and scientific methods

Definition

Psychology is the scientific study of behavior and mental processes and how they are affected by an organism's physical state, mental state, and external environment.

Scope of Study

  • Behavior and mental processes include overt, observable instances but also include subtle kinds of instances, such as brain activity.
  • The study extends to humans and many other creatures.
  • Physical state relates primarily to the organism's biology — most especially the state of the brain and central nervous system.
  • Mental state does not have to be conscious — it can be studied in many creatures without their conscious awareness, and can be examined in terms of brain activity.
  • All organisms function in an environment that is constantly presenting them with problems and challenges that must be solved.

Requirements of Scientific Study

Scientific study requires several foundational elements:

  1. Theoretical framework
  2. Testable hypotheses
  3. Empirical evidence

What is Islamic Psychology?

Islamic redefinition, implications, and soul-centred approach

Islamic Redefinition of Psychology

Islamic Psychology is a scientific study of the "manifestation" of the souls in the form of behaviour and mental processes.
  • Still uses scientific methods
  • Still focuses on behaviour and mental processes
  • Accepts the existence of the soul (ruh)
  • Uses the soul as a theoretical explanation

Implications of the Islamic Redefinition

  • Does not neglect the influence of biological, dispositional, and socio-cultural factors
  • Leads to a broader and more accurate model of human nature
  • Leads to a better understanding of the purpose of human creation

Preparing to Study Psychology: Qur'anic Framework

Lessons from Surah al-Fatihah and early Surah al-Baqarah

5.1 Surah Al-Fatihah as a Framework

Al-Qur'an is a book of knowledge and wisdom. There must be some lessons for human beings in why Allah begins a book full of knowledge and wisdom with Surah al-Fatihah. These lessons should be applied whenever we are seeking for knowledge and wisdom from other sources given by Allah.

Before We Study Psychology, We Must…

  • Have a sahih cognitive belief ('aqidah) in terms of tawhid rububiyyah (AQ 1:2) and uluhiyyah (AQ 1:5)
  • Have khushu' affective feelings in terms of love (AQ 1:3), fear (AQ 1:4), and hope (AQ 1:6)
  • Have distinct observable behaviours that differentiate us from non-hidayah groups (AQ 1:7)
  • Sincerely want to gain hidayah in studying psychology

5.2 Early Surah Al-Baqarah as a Compass

  • Accept that Al-Qur'an is the ultimate source to find the truth about psychology (AQ 2:2)
  • Strive to be in the group of muttaqin because only muttaqin will gain hidayah (AQ 2:2–5)
  • Strive to avoid being the group of kuffar (AQ 2:6–7) and munafiqin (AQ 2:8–20)
  • The variables that differentiate the three groups are the soul — manifested in the forms of behaviours and mental processes

The Soul and Human Complexity

The rulings, stories, arguments, etc. given in the al-Qur'an ALL contain hidayah messages for our souls — on how to behave and think (mental processes), and sometimes why we behave or think in certain ways.

Because of the existence of the soul (body × spirit), human beings are the most complex creation. It is very easy to be inaccurate (and sometimes un-Islamic) in social scientific theories, research, and applications.

Sources of Psychological Knowledge in Islam

Although al-Qur'an is the ultimate source for psychological knowledge, this does not mean that al-Qur'an is the sole source.

Reasons Why Al-Qur'an Is Not the Sole Source:

  • Qur'an is not a book of psychology — it is a book of hidayah
  • Qur'an urged us to study our own souls/selves
  • Hidayah can be gained through:
    • Readable āyāt maqrū'ah — signs of al-Qur'an
    • Observable āyāt manẓūrah — signs found in creation
